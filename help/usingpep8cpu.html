Using Pep/8 CPU

This CPU simulator is an implementation of the data section of the Pep/8 central processing unit as shown in Figure 12.2 of the textbook Computer Systems, J. Stanley Warford, Fourth edition, Jones and Bartlett, Publishers, 2010.

The simulator has two modes of operation — interactive and microcode. The application starts up in the interactive mode, which you can see by the fact that the two buttons Clock and Copy to Microcode are enabled and the two buttons Single Step and Resume are disabled.

You switch from interactive to microcode mode by entering a microcode program in the Microcode panel and selecting System->Start Debugging from the menu or clicking the corresponding Start Debugging icon on the toolbar.

When you are in microcode mode, the two buttons Clock and Copy to Microcode are disabled and the two buttons Single Step and Resume are enabled.

You exit the microcode mode in one of three ways — by running a microcode sequence to completion, by selecting System->Stop Debugging from the menu, or by clicking the corresponding Stop Debugging icon on the toolbar.
